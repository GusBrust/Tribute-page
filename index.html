Dietrich Bonhoeffer:
Pastor, Martyr, Prophet, Spy

"There is no one who has believed in God and the kingdom of God, there is no one who has heard of the kingdom of the resurrected and does not feel, from then on, an immense longing for home, a joyful anticipation for the liberation from bodily existence [...]. If our faith does not transform it, death is hell, it is night and cold. But this is precisely what is wonderful, the fact that we can transform death." Dietrich Bonhoeffer, in a sermon preached in London, November 1933

Here's a time line of Dietrich Bonhoeffer's life:

  • 1906 - Born in Breslau, Germany (now Wroclaw, Poland), on February 4.
  • 1923 - Completed his Abitur (university entrance exam) at the Friedrichs-Gymnasium in Berlin.
  • 1924 - Began studying theology at the University of Tübingen.
  • 1927 - Earned his Doctor of Theology degree from the University of Berlin with a dissertation titled "Sanctorum Communio" (Communion of Saints).
  • 1928 - Became an assistant pastor in Barcelona, Spain.
  • 1930 - Attended Union Theological Seminary in New York City, where he formed a friendship with African-American theologian Frank Fisher and was influenced by the social gospel movement.
  • 1931 - Returned to Germany and began lecturing in systematic theology at the University of Berlin. Ordained as a pastor in the Lutheran Church.
  • 1933 - Hitler came to power in Germany. Bonhoeffer became a vocal critic of the Nazi regime, delivering a radio broadcast critical of Hitler.
  • 1934 - Helped form the Confessing Church in opposition to the pro-Nazi "German Christians" faction within the Protestant Church.
  • 1935 - Appointed head of an underground seminary for the Confessing Church in Finkenwalde, which was eventually closed by the Gestapo in 1937.
  • 1937 - Published "The Cost of Discipleship," which argued against cheap grace and called for a costly discipleship.
  • 1939 - Visited the United States again but returned to Germany despite the risks, feeling a duty to his country and the church.
  • 1940 - Forbidden by the Nazis from public speaking and publishing.
  • 1943 - Arrested by the Gestapo on April 5 for his involvement in a plot to overthrow Hitler and for helping Jews escape to Switzerland.
  • 1944 - Imprisoned at the Tegel military prison in Berlin.
  • 1945 - Transferred to the Buchenwald concentration camp and then to Flossenbürg concentration camp. Executed by hanging on April 9, just weeks before the end of World War II in Europe.
